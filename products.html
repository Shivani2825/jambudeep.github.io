Mahua(Madhuca Longifolia) belongs to family sapotaceae, is known for its sweet flowers which posses a lot of ethenic values among the trible people for the development of various fermented and non-fermented food products. The mahua flower is edible and is a food item for tribals. They use it to make syrup for medicinal purposes. Flowers are rich in total sugars, out of which reducing sugar are present in high amount. There are many varieties of food prepared fermented to produce the alcoholic drink mahua.The liquor produced from the flowers is largely colourless, opaque and not very strong. It is inexpensive and the production is largely done in home stills. In Ayurveda,Mahua flowers are considered as to be cooling agent, Carminative,galactagogue and astringent.It also reported to be benificial for heart,skin and eyes. Mahua flowers are also used to manufacture jam, the flowers of mahua tree are sun-dried; these sun-dried flowers are ground to flour and used to make various kinds of breads.
